HOW IT’S MADE

1

Harvesting & Cleaning

Olives are picked (by hand or machine) and then cleaned at the mill to remove leaves, twigs, and dirt.

2

Crushing (Milling)

Cleaned olives are crushed into a thick paste (pulp) using modern hammer mills or traditional stone mills, including the pits, skin, and flesh.

3

Malaxation (Kneading)

The paste is slowly mixed (stirred) in large tanks for about 30-60 minutes at low temperatures (cold extraction) to help the tiny oil droplets clump together.

4

Extraction (Separation)

  • Centrifugation (Modern): The paste is spun at high speeds in a horizontal decanter, separating the oil from the solids and vegetation water by density.
  • Pressing (Traditional): The paste is spread on mats, stacked, and then hydraulically pressed to squeeze out the oil.
5

Final Separation

A vertical centrifuge is often used to remove any remaining water from the oil.

6

Filtering & Storage

The oil may be filtered and then stored in stainless steel tanks, often under nitrogen, to protect it from oxygen before bottling.

7

Quality Testing

The oil undergoes chemical analysis and expert tasting to determine its grade (e.g., Extra Virgin).
